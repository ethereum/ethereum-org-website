# Builder “Blog” on eth.org

Client: Builder Acceleration (https://www.notion.so/Builder-Acceleration-349d9895554180158f53d8a59dbbbb1c?pvs=21)
Context: Add builder ‘blog’ to the http://ethereum.org /developers hub where the EF Builder Acceleration team can highlight Ethereum builder resources and developments. 
Created: April 21, 2026 9:26 AM
Status: Potential next sprint
Type: Ethdotorg roadmap

## Summary

Add a markdown-based builder blog to the [ethereum.org /developers hub](https://ethereum.org/developers/) where the EF Builder Acceleration team can highlight Ethereum builder resources and developments.

## Why/what?

**What**

- A markdown-based blog section featuring a "view all" page and a recent posts carousel on the developer hub. Blog pages templates should be able to support regular markdown, code snippets, and standard embedded media.
- Posts will help spread awareness of new builder resources, including highlighting new prototypes/demo apps, builder tools, educational platform updates, and developments.

**Why**

- Create an open and accessible pathway for the Builder Acceleration team to get resources into more builders’ hands
- Help make ethereum.org a topical, relevant resource for the latest dev tools and educational resources in the ecosystem
- Unify builder/acc resources that are maintained across various sites/domains into one go-to source for the latest updates, hosted on eth-org

**Authoring & amplification**

- Posts will be authored by individual members of the EF Builder Success team
    - Consider current [Developer Tutorial author attribution](https://ethereum.org/developers/tutorials/gasless/). Blog posts should clearly attribute [author] and ‘EF Builder Success team’. The overall builder blog section on eth-org will not be specifically focused on EF team promotion (akin to the [Ethereum Community Hubs section](https://ethereum.org/community/events/) managed by EE team).
    - Using author persona JSON schemas (introduced in [PR #17988](https://github.com/ethereum/ethereum-org-website/pull/17988)) should make this possible & flexible for role changes over time, while supporting EEAT goals.
- External community submissions are unlikely but will be evaluated against site contribution guidelines if they occur
- Posts will be shared on socials by the builder success team and @ethdotorg

**Merge process**

- The builder success team will submit posts via the open repository
- Note - Digital Studio dev should establish a quick-turn merge process, which may need to be handled separately from standard site release schedules.
