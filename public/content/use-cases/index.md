Ethereum is a platform for building applications that run without a central authority. People use it for financial tools, digital ownership, governance, scientific funding, and more. These are not theoretical possibilities. They are live applications used by millions of people around the world today.

This page organizes the main use cases into categories. Each links to a dedicated page with more detail.

## Financial tools {#financial-tools}

Ethereum enables open financial services that anyone can access with an internet connection. There are no banks to approve accounts, no brokers to take a cut, and no borders limiting who can participate.

<CardGrid>

- **[Decentralized finance (DeFi)](/defi/)**: Lend, borrow, trade, and earn interest without intermediaries. DeFi gives you direct control over your financial activity.
- **[Stablecoins](/stablecoins/)**: Tokens pegged to stable assets like the US dollar. Stablecoins combine the stability of traditional currencies with the speed and openness of Ethereum.
- **[Payments](/payments/)**: Send money anywhere in the world in minutes with low fees. Ethereum makes cross-border payments fast and accessible.
- **[Real-world assets (RWAs)](/real-world-assets/)**: Tokenized versions of real estate, bonds, commodities, and other traditional assets. RWAs bring liquidity and accessibility to markets that were previously hard to access.

</CardGrid>

## Digital ownership and gaming {#digital-ownership-and-gaming}

Ethereum lets you own digital items in a way that is verifiable, transferable, and independent of any company. This applies to art, collectibles, in-game items, and more.

<CardGrid>

- **[Non-fungible tokens (NFTs)](/nft/)**: Unique digital items with provable ownership. NFTs let creators sell directly to audiences and let collectors hold verifiable originals.
- **[Gaming](/gaming/)**: Games where you truly own your in-game assets and where game logic runs on open, transparent smart contracts.

</CardGrid>

## Organizations and identity {#organizations-and-identity}

Ethereum provides tools for collective decision-making, self-sovereign identity, and social platforms that users control.

<CardGrid>

- **[DAOs](/dao/)**: Internet-native organizations governed by their members through transparent voting. No single person controls the funds or rules.
- **[Decentralized identity](/decentralized-identity/)**: Own and control your digital identity without relying on centralized providers. Prove things about yourself without revealing more than necessary.
- **[Decentralized social networks](/social-networks/)**: Social platforms where you own your data, content, and connections. No company can deplatform you or sell your information.

</CardGrid>

## Science and public goods {#science-and-public-goods}

Ethereum is used to fund and coordinate research, environmental projects, and public goods in new ways.

<CardGrid>

- **[Decentralized science (DeSci)](/desci/)**: Open infrastructure for funding, publishing, and crediting scientific research. DeSci uses Ethereum to make science more transparent and accessible.
- **[Regenerative finance (ReFi)](/refi/)**: Financial tools designed to fund environmental and social regeneration rather than extraction.

</CardGrid>

## Emerging use cases {#emerging-use-cases}

New applications are constantly being built on Ethereum. These areas are rapidly growing and evolving.

<CardGrid>

- **[AI agents](/ai-agents/)**: Autonomous AI systems that can hold funds, execute transactions, and interact with smart contracts on your behalf.
- **[Prediction markets](/prediction-markets/)**: Markets where participants bet on the outcomes of future events, using financial incentives to generate crowd-sourced forecasts.
- **[Restaking](/restaking/)**: Extend Ethereum's security to other networks and services by reusing staked ETH, earning additional rewards while strengthening the ecosystem.

</CardGrid>
