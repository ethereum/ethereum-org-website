# Escaping Venezuela's currency collapse {#escaping-venezuelas-currency-collapse}

> This is a placeholder story used while the full Human Stories layout is being designed. The copy below is illustrative.

When the bolívar lost most of its value, holding cash meant watching savings evaporate week after week. For many Venezuelans, dollar-pegged stablecoins on Ethereum became a lifeline — a way to store value that the local currency could no longer provide.

## A way to save {#a-way-to-save}

With nothing more than a phone and an internet connection, people could hold value in a currency that didn't melt away overnight, and send it to family without relying on banks that had stopped working for them.

## Why it matters {#why-it-matters}

These are not abstract use cases. For people living through an economic collapse, open and permissionless money is the difference between keeping what they've earned and losing it.
